The APHG National Exam

The AP Human Geography natinoal exam is taken in May by all AP Human Geography students in the country (who registered to take it). The test consists of two parts: the multiple choice section and the free response section. Both sections are timed. The multiple choice section is 75 questions long with a 60-minute time limit. Points are not deducted for incorrect answers. The free response section consists of three essay prompts with a 75-minute time limit. Both of these sections are each worth half of the overall grade. All AP exams are graded on a scale of 1 to 5, with 1 being the lowest and 5 being the highest. In order to pass the exam and get credit for the class, you must get either a 3, 4, or 5 on it. Some colleges, however, only except a 4 or a 5. Check with the colleges you're interested in for the best information.

Strategies for Answering Exam Multiple-Choice Questions

Remember that on the actual AP exam, because there is so much information, some of the questions will be unfamiliar to you. If you, however, review all the material you learned with this app (Learn It), answer the mock multiple-choice questions (Review It), and use the general strategies presented here (Take It), you will greatly increase your chances of getting the right answer and Acing the AP!

(4) Don't forget about the time limit!

Pace yourself. Remember, you have only 60 minutes to answer the 75 multiple choice questions, which calculates out to be less than a minute per question. Skip the harder questions and come back to them later.

(5) Relax.

Don't stress about the test. Sleep early the night before and have a good breakfast the day of the test. Stay calm during the test, hurrying will make you nervous and will result in a lower score.
